Enterprise Data Access & Relationship Graph — a control plane that asks "who's allowed?" before "what's connected?"

Your company's map of systems, access, and data stays inside your own walls — and every action must earn its way through these gates:

🪪
Step 1 · Identity

Prove who you are

"Who is asking?"
Your real company login (Okta, Microsoft). Nobody gets to claim an identity — or skip MFA.
→
🗺️
Step 2 · Access Graph

Prove a path exists

"Are you actually allowed to reach this?"
A live map of who can reach what — through groups, roles, and permissions. No path, no access.
→
⚖️
Step 3 · Policy

Check the rules

"Should that access be honored right now?"
Clear, versioned rules: MFA? Risky session? Production? Need a ticket? Need a human approval?
→
🔑
Step 4 · Broker

Issue a temporary key

"Access for minutes, not forever."
A short-lived key scoped to exactly this one task. It expires on its own — no standing privilege.
→
🧩
Step 5 · Context

See only your slice

"What is this thing connected to?"
The object map answers — but only the approved neighborhood. Sensitive items stay hidden.
→
🛠️
Step 6 · Action

Do the governed thing

"Approved verbs only."
A fixed menu of safe actions. Risky ones run in a sealed box that never hands out the keys.
→
📜
Step 7 · Audit

Record everything

"Who, what, why, under which rule?"
A tamper-evident log of every decision — each entry sealed to the one before it.
🤖
Local AI Feedback Loop
A private AI (running on your infrastructure) studies the audit trail and suggests improvements — simpler approvals, unused access to remove, better rules. Humans approve every change. The AI never enforces anything.
